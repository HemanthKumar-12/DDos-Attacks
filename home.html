    Identify and stop the attacks





    Normal

    The term Normal refers to standard network activities and behaviors that do not involve any unusual or malicious actions. This is the baseline against which anomalies and attacks are measured.

    DoS/DDoS

    A Denial of Service (DoS) or Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack aims to disrupt the normal functioning of a network or service by overwhelming it with excessive traffic. This prevents legitimate users from accessing the targeted resource.

    Port Scan

    A Port Scan is a method used by attackers to discover open ports and services on a networked device. By scanning different ports, an attacker can identify potential vulnerabilities to exploit.

    Botnet

    A Botnet is a network of compromised computers controlled by an attacker. These bots can be used to perform coordinated attacks, send spam, or steal data without the knowledge of the device owners.

    Infiltration

    Infiltration involves gaining unauthorized access to a network or system. This can be achieved through various means, including exploiting vulnerabilities, social engineering, or insider threats.

    Web Attack

    A Web Attack targets websites and web applications with the aim of exploiting vulnerabilities to gain unauthorized access, steal data, or disrupt services. Common types include SQL injection, cross-site scripting (XSS), and cross-site request forgery (CSRF).

    Brute Force

    Brute Force attacks involve systematically trying all possible combinations of passwords or encryption keys until the correct one is found. This method relies on computational power and can be time-consuming but effective against weak passwords.
